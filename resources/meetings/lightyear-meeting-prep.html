Key Talking Points

For John (PE value creation + technical credibility)

  • Speak his language: He came from Vista — the gold standard for PE operational excellence. Frame Acceler as the AI-era equivalent of Vista's operational playbook, but delivered as a service rather than built in-house.
  • Don't trash McKinsey, but differentiate: "Consulting firms are great at identifying what to do. The gap is in who actually does it. We embed practitioners who build and ship." He'll agree from personal experience.
  • Cross-portfolio leverage: "The patterns we see in PracticeTek — AI for intake, scribing, billing — are the same patterns in Vehlo's auto repair shops and CuraLinc's mental health intake. Once we crack it for one, it compounds across the portfolio."
  • He understands ML: You can be specific. Don't hand-wave about "AI." Talk about agent design, workflow orchestration, human-in-the-loop evaluation, fine-tuning where appropriate.

For Doug (Compliance & governance)

  • Regulated industries: Their portfolio is heavy financial services and healthcare. AI in these sectors touches PII, PHI, SOC 2, HIPAA, SEC requirements. Mention that your approach includes governance and compliance frameworks.
  • Human-in-the-loop: Emphasize that AI-native workflows include human oversight, especially in regulated contexts. Not about replacing people, but augmenting them with guardrails.
  • Risk framing: AI deployment without governance is a risk. But NOT deploying AI while competitors do is also a risk. Position Acceler as reducing both risks simultaneously.

General

  • The GSD positioning: Michal specifically said the gap is "GSD people" who own execution end-to-end. This is your differentiator. Reinforce it: "We're not here to write reports. We embed practitioners who ship real systems."
  • Low friction: The free workshop is the key ask. Don't try to close a big deal. The goal is to get in front of 2–3 portfolio company CEOs and demonstrate value.
  • They're early innings: Michal said they're in awareness mode, not deployment mode. Meet them where they are — the diagnostic and workshop are the right entry points, not co-build.

Questions to Ask

  1. "Where are your portfolio companies on the AI spectrum? Who's furthest ahead and who's struggling?"
  2. "When you think about AI across the portfolio, is the bigger concern competitive disruption risk or operational efficiency opportunity?"
  3. "How does your Portfolio Solutions team currently engage with companies on technology and process improvement?"
  4. "Have you done any AI readiness assessments across the portfolio? If so, what did you find?"
  5. "What would a successful pilot look like from your perspective — what would need to be true for you to expand it?"
  6. "Which 2–3 portfolio companies would be the best candidates to start with? Who are the CEOs most receptive to this?"

Objections to Prepare For

"We're already working with McKinsey/BCG on AI."

Response: "That's great for the diagnostic and strategy layer. What we've seen is the gap is in execution — the people who actually build and deploy the AI-native workflows. We're complementary, not competitive. We're the team that takes the McKinsey recommendation and makes it real."

"Our portfolio companies don't have budget for this."

Response: "That's exactly why we start with a free workshop. Zero cost, zero commitment. If the leadership team doesn't find it valuable, no harm done. If they do, we structure the engagement to be outcome-aligned — we get paid when the company sees results."

"We tried bringing in AI consultants before and it didn't work."

Response: "That's the most common thing we hear. Most AI consultants deliver decks, not systems. Our practitioners come from Meta, Google, Amazon — they've built the AI systems at scale. They embed with your teams and ship working solutions, not PowerPoints."

"How is this different from what we could build internally?"

Response: "You could, but the math is hard: hiring even 2–3 top AI practitioners is $2M+/year. We give you access to 700+ practitioners on-demand, deployed against specific portfolio company needs. It's the Vista model — specialist capability as a service, not a fixed cost."

"AI moves so fast, how do we know what you teach today is relevant tomorrow?"

Response: "That's exactly why we use active practitioners, not academics. Our people are building with the latest tools at the frontier companies right now. When the models change, our practitioners already know how to use them because they helped build them."

Success Criteria

The meeting is a success if you walk away with:

  1. Agreement on 2–3 target portfolio companies to explore (ideally PracticeTek + one other)
  2. A concrete next step with a date — either a follow-up call with a portfolio company CEO, or scheduling the free workshop
  3. Clarity on John's role — is he the person who would sponsor this across the portfolio? Does he need to bring it to an investment committee?

Remember: You cannot rely on PE alone to drive adoption. Even if John and Doug love this, you still need to sell to and prove value for the portfolio company exec teams. The goal of this meeting is to get in front of those teams.
